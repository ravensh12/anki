# Ante — learning science map

Every feature traces to evidence and to one of the four design principles.
If a feature serves none of them, it is cut (PRD 1.2). This document is the
"reasoning" layer: what each mechanism is, the science behind it, and where it
lives in the code.

## The four design principles (PRD 1.2)

1. **When > how much.** Schedule is the lever, not raw hours → spaced scheduler;
   reject "hours logged" as a metric.
2. **System, not willpower.** Make the correct next action the path of least
   resistance → due-stack, micro-sessions, cue-anchored notifications.
3. **Covered ≠ known.** Coverage is what you walked past; mastery is what you can
   retrieve → topic mastery-gating, corrective routing, mastery map.
4. **Honesty over encouragement.** Students fear not knowing, not bad news →
   calibrated readiness, abstention, and willingness to show what is _not_
   learned. Honesty leads; motivation supports it. The only _banned_ metrics are
   the hollow ones (pure login/attendance streaks, leaderboards). A bounded
   extrinsic layer (opt-in, effort-gated, mastery-linked, surprise-not-wage) is
   permitted _beside_ the honest signals because the evidence says unexpected,
   competence-carrying, autonomy-supported rewards don't crowd out intrinsic
   drive — and it is instrumented so it can be cut if it ever does.

## Evidence-honesty notes (what we do NOT overclaim)

- We cite mastery learning at the **defensible ~0.5 SD** (Kulik 1990), never
  Bloom's contested 2-sigma headline.
- The consumer promise is an **efficiency** claim ("equal retention from less
  time"), never a guaranteed score — a guarantee would contradict Principle 4.
- Anki correlations with Step scores are confounded by conscientiousness; we use
  Kerfoot's RCTs for causal language and treat Anki data as supporting.
