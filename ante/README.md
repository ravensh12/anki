# ANTE — a members-only card den for the MCAT, forked on Anki

**Exam: MCAT** (total 472–528; four sections each 118–132: C/P, CARS, B/B, P/S).

> You open Ante seated in **the Emerald Room**, a card den above Canal Street.
> **Sahir**, a djinn who has dealt cards since Babylon, deals your due cards
> onto green felt. Your opponent is **the House — the forgetting curve** — and
> the House always wins unless you show up. Anki tells you whether you remember
> a card. Ante does the three things Anki structurally cannot: it **gates
> progress on demonstrated topic mastery** (tables are _won_, not visited), it
> measures the **memory→performance gap** (can you use a fact on a novel
> MCAT-style question, not just recall it), and it posts an **honest,
> calibrated readiness line that abstains when it lacks evidence** ("NO LINE"
> beats a guess in a nice font).

The spacing engine inherited from Anki (FSRS) is table stakes; these three
layers are the product. The consumer promise is **time back**: the same
retention from roughly half the seat time, because the schedule — not extra
hours — does the work.

AGPL-3.0-or-later fork of [Anki](https://github.com/ankitects/anki) (Ankitects
Pty Ltd); some Anki components are BSD-3-Clause.

## The world — the den and the Circuit

Ante's interface is a place, not a dashboard (`ante/circuit.py`,
`ante/web/den.html`):

- **The Emerald Room** — the home screen. The windows keep your real clock
  (dawn behind the glass until the morning game is kept; neon rain at the
  midnight game). Sahir waits at the felt. Exactly one chair is ever pulled
  out — **the Seat**, the single best next action (Principle 2).
- **The Circuit** — the mastery map as a world tour. Each MCAT section is a
  city (New York, Monte Carlo, Havana, Macau); every topic is a **table** in
  that city. The engine's honest signals set each table's state: _roped off_
  (locked behind prerequisites), _open_ (you have a seat), _the low table_
  (corrective), _won_ (mastered — the plaque is yours), _unlisted_ (no
  evidence; the Circuit refuses to list what it can't support). **Dust
  settles over a won table as FSRS retrievability decays** — the forgetting
  curve as the House quietly reclaiming the room. Beat every city and the
  **Final Table** — a rooftop at first light, exam day — opens.
- **The Table** — the session. Cards come off the shoe in **points-at-stake
  order** (the Rust engine change); the chip stack riding each card _is_ its
  live stake. Before a card turns you **Check / Call / Raise** (pre-flip
  confidence, feeding calibration); a lapse animates the House raking the
  pot; every third hand is **the river** — an application item, the thing
  that actually moves the Circuit. The cash-out shows the honest tally, never
  fake winnings.
- **The Book** — readiness as the house oddsmaker. Past the evidence floor it
  posts the line (range + confidence + reasons); below it the board reads
  **NO LINE — insufficient action**. Your **tell** (self-trust) shows whether
  your own read can be trusted; saying "sure" and missing pulls the line down.
- **The Back Room** — the oral defense, heads-up with Sahir. Explain a topic
  straight (typed, spoken in takes, or — with an OpenAI key — **live**: a real
  streaming voice conversation where he probes, interrupts, and delivers the
  verdict aloud). The honesty line holds even in live mode: **the voice only
  performs; a deterministic rubric grades every turn** (`create_response` is
  off — after each turn the page grades the transcript through the same path
  as a typed answer, then hands Sahir a private ledger cue). Win and the table
  is yours outright.
- **The Vault** — bespoke mnemonic scenes commissioned for exactly the cards
  your memory keeps rejecting (measured leeches), rendered by the Studio.
- **The Midnight Game** — the day's two bookends, plus **the last hand,
  replayed** (tonight's hardest retrievals, replayed slow before sleep) and,
  on exam eve, **The Run** — your documentary, cut only from logged history.

## The Studio — every visual is Higgsfield, pre-built and curated

The den's cinema comes from a bounded, hand-curated asset library generated by
`ante/tools/gen_world.py` (Higgsfield **Soul** stills, **DoP** living loops,
ElevenLabs dealer voice lines) into `ante/web/assets/`. Sahir is locked by one
fixed character prompt reused verbatim plus curation (`--take N` rerolls a
drifted shot). Three hard rules:

1. **Card text is never baked into generated media.** Plates are backdrops;
   the cards stay crisp HTML composited on top.
2. **Everything degrades gracefully.** With no keys, the room is drawn in CSS
   and the offline engraver produces deterministic felt plates — the den is
   never blank, just quieter.
3. **The camera never wanders.** Every DoP loop is pinned to one curated
   camera-motion preset (`HF_DOP_MOTION`, a locked-off tripod move) and every
   motion prompt bans film rigs in frame and rain indoors — the two artifacts
   that once shipped (a camera drifting through the felt; rain falling inside
   the room). Curated takes are trimmed to their clean frames and looped as
   palindromes; `gen_world` writes each loop's VP9 `.webm` twin automatically
   (Anki's QtWebEngine has no H.264). The `den_day` plate is benched until
   regenerated (`--scene den_day --motion --take 1`) — by day the room falls
   back to its built-in CSS scene rather than show rain indoors.

### The cold open — the film that proves the product

`den.html`'s film plays at every sign-in — eight shots, two acts and a
close: the hook (Sahir deals real premed cards), **the walkthrough** — a
hand plays itself under a ghost cursor (Check/Call/Raise → turn → grade),
the answer is traced into FSRS and back out as the re-deal schedule (why
cards repeat), and the mastery gate shows that **only quizzes and open
answers win tables** while flashcard self-ratings only schedule reviews —
then **the receipts**: Roediger & Karpicke 2006 (tested 61% vs re-read 40%,
one week out), Cepeda 2006 (839 assessments), Bahrick 1993, Dunlosky 2013,
drawn as animated charts before the logo close. Dealer lines
`vo_film_1..8.mp3` pace the cut; with no audio the timers cover it, and
choreographed shots carry a duration floor so a short line can never cut
the animation. **The whole cast is one actor per render**: `Studio.speech`
keys its cache on the resolved engine + voice, so a keyless ElevenLabs run
falls back to OpenAI's dealer for *every* line rather than splicing two
voices into one film (re-render with `gen_world --voice` after changing
keys and the full set recasts together).

Per-student media (Vault scenes, heads-up verdict clips, reel narration) render
through the same provider-isolated engine (`ante/ai/studio.py`: Higgsfield
Soul/DoP/Speak v2, ElevenLabs, OpenAI) when keys are present (`HF_KEY`,
`ELEVENLABS_API_KEY`, `OPENAI_API_KEY`) — content-addressed, budget-capped,
and fully functional with AI switched off.

## The four design principles

Every feature traces to one; if it serves none, it's cut. Full evidence map:
[`docs/learning-science.md`](docs/learning-science.md).

1. **When > how much** — schedule is the lever → spaced scheduler; reject "hours".
2. **System, not willpower** — make the next correct action the path of least
   resistance → the Seat, micro-sessions, cue-anchored nudges.
3. **Covered ≠ known** — a table is won by **application (quizzes +
   open-ended), not flashcard self-ratings**; corrective routing to the low
   table; the Circuit.
4. **Honesty over encouragement** — calibrated readiness, abstention ("NO
   LINE"), and a tell that drops when confidence outruns accuracy. Casino
   aesthetics, no casino dishonesty: chips visualize the engine's real
   points-at-stake signal, and the cash-out never invents winnings. A
   **bounded, opt-in** motivation layer (effort-gated run + freezes +
   _surprise_ rewards) rides beside the honest signals, engineered against
   the overjustification effect (Deci/Ryan 1999) and measured so it can be cut.

## Architecture (desktop)

```
Rust engine (rslib)  ── points-at-stake order + GetTopicMastery RPC
   │  single seam: Backend::run_service_method(service, method, bytes)
   ├─ Python (pylib + ante/) ── mastery-gating, models, coverage, sessions,
   │                            intentions, rewards (all dependency-free,
   │                            run in-process so the app scores with AI OFF)
   ├─ Web UI (mediasrv) ── /_anki/ante — the den (den.html): the Table +
   │                       the Circuit + the Book + the Back Room
   └─ Model/AI service (ante/service, FastAPI) ── ONLINE-ONLY: AI card
                                generation (RAG) + eval; never required to score
```

- **Engine change** (required): points-at-stake order + mastery query.
  Rationale + merge-risk: [`docs/rust-change.md`](docs/rust-change.md).
- **Mastery-gating** (the differentiator): `ante/mastery.py` — four states
  (locked/active/corrective/mastered) gated on **demonstrated application**
  (quiz + open-ended accuracy ≥ `MASTERY_BAR`), _not_ flashcard self-ratings;
  the unlock graph from `data/mcat_outline.json`. FSRS card-strength stays a
  separate retention signal (set `mastery_requires_strength` to re-add it).

## The three scores (separate, honest)

| Score       | Question                 | Module                                                                             |
| ----------- | ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Memory      | Recall now?              | `memory.py` (FSRS + calibration: Brier/log-loss/ECE + range)                       |
| Performance | New exam question right? | `performance.py` (bridge; beats memory baseline; paraphrase test)                  |
| Readiness   | Score today?             | `readiness.py` (per-topic → section 118–132 → total, range + confidence + reasons) |

One page each on how these work (inputs, method, uncertainty, limits) is in
[`docs/model-cards.md`](docs/model-cards.md); captured proof numbers for every
graded claim are in [`docs/results.md`](docs/results.md).

**Give-up rule (enforced):** no line until enough graded reviews **and**
weighted coverage with no high-weight blind spot. Below the floor the Book
shows what it can and says it cannot yet post. "A confident number with
nothing behind it is a guess in a nice font." Past the floor the Book also
reports **how accurate its past lines turned out to be** against completed
full-lengths (`trackrecord.py`) — honesty about its own track record.

## Personalized by your exam date — the tournament clock

Onboarding asks **one** thing first — _when do you sit?_ — then a target, a
seat portrait, and the **buy-in game** (the baseline diagnostic), and
recomputes the whole system (`ante/recalibrate.py`):

- **Daily minutes** = remaining mastery work ÷ study-days-left + review
  upkeep, scaled by a bounded target-climb factor from your buy-in.
- **The blinds rise**: FSRS desired retention ramps up as the exam nears
  (Cepeda 2008) and review intervals are capped at test day.
- **Shape of the day** places new/hard work in your chronotype's peak window
  and light review right before sleep (consolidation).

## The daily ritual — the morning game & the midnight game

The day is bracketed by two short, high-leverage games (`ante/ritual.py`):
the **morning game**, cold recall before coffee (testing beats rereading),
and the **midnight game**, one light hand your brain banks overnight. The den
shows both as chips by the window, with no-shame copy; they're what the run
counts.

## When-to-study nudges — even when the app is closed (Principle 2)

`ante/reminders.py` computes the cue-anchored, no-shame schedule;
`qt/aqt/ante_reminders.py` delivers it as a **native macOS Notification
Center banner** (tray/toast elsewhere) while the app is open. Opt in to
**background nudges** and `ante/os_notify.py` registers the morning/midnight
cues with the OS scheduler (launchd / Task Scheduler / systemd) so the
morning game opens like an alarm clock before you've opened anything.
Reversible, quiet-hours-safe. Full design:
[`docs/notifications.md`](docs/notifications.md).

## Demo mode

A self-advancing tour from the door (or House Rules → settings):
`ante/tools/seed_demo.py` seeds a realistic history so the den demos as a live
instrument, with named jumps (First night → The Bridge → The Sharpen → Final

## Build & run

Prereqs: `just`, Rust toolchain, `n2` (`bash tools/install-n2`).

```bash
just run                 # build + launch desktop (web served at :40000)
# the den: http://localhost:40000/_anki/ante
```

**Content is premade — there is nothing to import.** The full high-yield MCAT
deck (`ante/data/seed_cards.json`, every AAMC-outline topic) self-seeds into the
collection on first launch (`aqt.ante.ensure_seed_deck`), so the tables are
dealt from the very first session. `just seed-deck` just exports that same deck
as a standalone `.anki2`/`.apkg` for benchmarking.

### Ante commands

```bash
just seed-deck                    # export the premade MCAT deck (+ .apkg); the app self-seeds it on first run
just seed-deck 1400               # a 50k-card deck for scale benchmarking
just bench out/mcat_seed.anki2    # engine p50/p95/worst per action (+ cold start, RSS)
just experiment --days 14         # mastery-gating vs ablation vs plain Anki
just ai-eval --offline --source <chapter.txt>   # AI eval vs keyword/TF-IDF baselines
just calibrate                    # memory calibration: Brier/log-loss/ECE + reliability.svg
just paraphrase                   # the paraphrase test (7d): 30 cards x 2 reworded questions
just leakage-check out/leak_train.json out/leak_test.json   # train/test contamination scan (7e)
just crash-test                   # 20x SIGKILL mid-review -> zero corruption (7g)
just installer                    # build the desktop installer -> out/installer/dist/

# the den's cinematic asset library (Higgsfield; curated, checked in):
PYTHONPATH=. out/pyenv/bin/python -m ante.tools.gen_world --scene all --motion
PYTHONPATH=. out/pyenv/bin/python -m ante.tools.gen_world --voice

# optional online AI service (committed FastAPI stack):
just ante-service-setup           # one-time: create venv + install fastapi/sklearn/anthropic
just ante-service                 # uvicorn ante.service.app:app
```

## AI (card generation)

Provider-isolated: **Claude** when `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY` + SDK are present, else
a deterministic **offline** provider. Pipeline: chunk a named source →
**retrieve** relevant chunks (TF-IDF, beats a keyword baseline) → generate →
**check** against a 50-pair gold set with a pre-declared cutoff → emit only
passing cards. Every card carries a traceable source quote + span; a
prompt-injection guard fences sources; `leakage.py` scans for train/test
contamination. The app always scores with AI off.

## Tunable constants

In `ante/config.py` (override via `ANTE_<NAME>` env): `STRENGTH_FRACTION`
0.85, `R_THRESHOLD` 0.90, `MASTERY_BAR` 0.80, `GIVEUP_MIN_REVIEWS`,
`GIVEUP_MIN_COVERAGE`, `DEFAULT_SESSION_MINUTES` 10, `AI_CARD_CUTOFF` 0.60,
`test_out_enabled` (engagement guardrail).

## Honesty notes (what we do NOT claim)

- Readiness is a documented **heuristic** map, not a validated AAMC
  concordance; we grade the bridge steps and abstain rather than fake a line.
- The study-feature experiment is a **simulation harness**; it reports nulls
  honestly and accepts real review logs in place of the learner model.
- The consumer promise is **efficiency** ("equal retention from less time"),
  never a guaranteed score.
